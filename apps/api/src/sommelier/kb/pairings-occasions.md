# Pairings & occasions guide

How to match our dishes to the occasion, the appetite, and the party size. As in
the taste guide, dishes are named exactly as they appear on the menu, and no
prices, discounts, or unavailable items are described here.

## Sharing and party size

- **For two people / a date / "something to share"** — the **Couple’s Set** is
  built for two: twenty pieces balanced across nigiri, maki, and sashimi, so two
  guests get range without ordering several dishes. This is the natural answer to
  "what's good for two?" or "a set for sharing".
- **For one person who wants variety** — the **Chef’s Omakase** is a twelve-piece
  chef-selected course; a single guest gets a guided range of the kitchen's best
  cuts without choosing each piece.
- **For a quick, single, satisfying plate** — the **Ikura Don** is a complete
  rice bowl: one dish, no assembly, comforting and filling on its own.

## Appetite and richness level

- **Want to go all-out / a special indulgence** — lead with the **Otoro
  Selection** (the richest single item) or the **Toro Truffle Roll** (rich plus
  truffle aroma). Pair either with a clean dish like the **Sashimi Moriawase** to
  balance the fat with something lighter.
- **Want something lighter overall** — the **Sashimi Moriawase** keeps the meal
  clean and delicate; the **Ikura Don** adds warm rice without heaviness.
- **Can't decide** — a chef-selected option (**Chef’s Omakase**) or a shared set
  (**Couple’s Set**) removes the choice and still covers a spread of flavors.

## Balancing an order

A satisfying order often pairs one rich, fatty dish with one clean, fresh dish so
the palate resets between bites:

- **Otoro Selection** (rich) + **Sashimi Moriawase** (clean) — a classic
  contrast of buttery and delicate.
- **Toro Truffle Roll** (rich, aromatic) + **Ikura Don** (briny, warm rice) —
  truffle-and-tuna depth against bright, popping roe.

When a guest already names one dish, suggest a complementary second from the
opposite end of the richness scale rather than two dishes that taste alike.

## Drinks

We are a sushi shop and do not list specific beverages on the menu, so a
recommendation should not promise a particular drink by name. In general terms,
green tea is a clean, traditional accompaniment that suits both the rich and the
delicate dishes above; if a guest asks specifically what to drink, it is honest
to suggest green tea as a classic pairing while noting that drink selection is
not part of the menu.
